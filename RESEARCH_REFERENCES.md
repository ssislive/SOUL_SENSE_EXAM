## Foundational Works on Emotional Intelligence

### Core Theoretical Frameworks

1. **Salovey, P., & Mayer, J. D. (1990).** "Emotional Intelligence." _Imagination, Cognition and Personality_, 9(3), 185-211.

   - **DOI**: 10.2190/DUGG-P24E-52WK-6CDG
   - **Summary**: Original definition of emotional intelligence as a form of social intelligence involving the ability to monitor one's own and others' feelings and emotions, to discriminate among them, and to use this information to guide thinking and actions.
   - **Relevance**: Foundational framework for EI assessment methodology.

2. **Mayer, J. D., & Salovey, P. (1997).** "What is emotional intelligence?" In P. Salovey & D. J. Sluyter (Eds.), _Emotional development and emotional intelligence: Educational implications_ (pp. 3-34). Basic Books.

   - **ISBN**: 978-0465095872
   - **Summary**: Refined four-branch model of EI: perceiving emotions, using emotions to facilitate thought, understanding emotions, and managing emotions.
   - **Relevance**: Theoretical basis for question categorization in Soul Sense.

3. **Goleman, D. (1995).** _Emotional Intelligence: Why It Can Matter More Than IQ_. Bantam Books.

   - **ISBN**: 978-0553383713
   - **Summary**: Popularized emotional intelligence concept, emphasizing five domains: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills.
   - **Relevance**: Public understanding and practical applications of EI.

4. **Mayer, J. D., Salovey, P., & Caruso, D. R. (2004).** "Emotional Intelligence: Theory, Findings, and Implications." _Psychological Inquiry_, 15(3), 197-215.

   - **DOI**: 10.1207/s15327965pli1503_02
   - **Summary**: Comprehensive review of EI research, clarifying ability model vs. mixed models, and empirical evidence for EI as a distinct construct.
   - **Relevance**: Validates EI as measurable psychological construct.

5. **Bar-On, R. (1997).** _Bar-On Emotional Quotient Inventory (EQ-i): Technical Manual_. Multi-Health Systems.
   - **Summary**: Introduced the term "Emotional Quotient" (EQ) and developed one of the first self-report EI measures.
   - **Relevance**: Established EQ terminology and self-report assessment approach.

## EI Assessment and Measurement

### Assessment Instruments and Methodologies

6. **Brackett, M. A., Rivers, S. E., Shiffman, S., Lerner, N., & Salovey, P. (2006).** "Relating Emotional Abilities to Social Functioning: A Comparison of Self-Report and Performance Measures of Emotional Intelligence." _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_, 91(4), 780-795.

   - **DOI**: 10.1037/0022-3514.91.4.780
   - **Summary**: Compared self-report and ability-based EI measures, finding distinct patterns of social and emotional functioning.
   - **Relevance**: Validates use of self-report measures for certain EI aspects.

7. **Petrides, K. V., & Furnham, A. (2001).** "Trait Emotional Intelligence: Psychometric Investigation with Reference to Established Trait Taxonomies." _European Journal of Personality_, 15(6), 425-448.

   - **DOI**: 10.1002/per.416
   - **Summary**: Distinguished trait EI (self-perceptions) from ability EI (actual abilities), proposing trait EI as distinct personality dimension.
   - **Relevance**: Theoretical justification for self-assessment approach in Soul Sense.

8. **Schutte, N. S., Malouff, J. M., Hall, L. E., Haggerty, D. J., Cooper, J. T., Golden, C. J., & Dornheim, L. (1998).** "Development and Validation of a Measure of Emotional Intelligence." _Personality and Individual Differences_, 25(2), 167-177.

   - **DOI**: 10.1016/S0191-8869(98)00001-4
   - **Summary**: Developed self-report measure based on Salovey and Mayer's model, demonstrating good reliability and validity.
   - **Relevance**: Methodological framework for questionnaire development.

9. **Joseph, D. L., & Newman, D. A. (2010).** "Emotional Intelligence: An Integrative Meta-Analysis and Cascading Model." _Journal of Applied Psychology_, 95(1), 54-78.

   - **DOI**: 10.1037/a0017286
   - **Summary**: Meta-analysis of 127 studies showing EI predicts job performance, with ability measures and self-report measures capturing different aspects.
   - **Relevance**: Validates predictive validity of EI assessments.

10. **O'Connor, R. M., Jr., & Little, I. S. (2003).** "Revisiting the Predictive Validity of Emotional Intelligence: Self-Report Versus Ability-Based Measures." _Personality and Individual Differences_, 35(8), 1893-1902.
    - **DOI**: 10.1016/S0191-8869(03)00038-2
    - **Summary**: Examined incremental validity of EI measures over personality and cognitive ability.
    - **Relevance**: Supports use of self-report EI measures for specific applications.

## Psychometric Properties and Validation

### Reliability, Validity, and Scale Development

11. **Cronbach, L. J. (1951).** "Coefficient Alpha and the Internal Structure of Tests." _Psychometrika_, 16(3), 297-334.

    - **DOI**: 10.1007/BF02310555
    - **Summary**: Introduced Cronbach's alpha for assessing internal consistency reliability.
    - **Relevance**: Standard for evaluating questionnaire reliability.

12. **Nunnally, J. C., & Bernstein, I. H. (1994).** _Psychometric Theory_ (3rd ed.). McGraw-Hill.

    - **ISBN**: 978-0070478497
    - **Summary**: Comprehensive text on measurement theory, reliability, and validity.
    - **Relevance**: Methodological foundation for test construction and validation.

13. **DeVellis, R. F. (2016).** _Scale Development: Theory and Applications_ (4th ed.). Sage Publications.

    - **ISBN**: 978-1506341569
    - **Summary**: Practical guide to developing psychological measurement scales.
    - **Relevance**: Guidelines for questionnaire item development and refinement.

14. **Messick, S. (1995).** "Validity of Psychological Assessment: Validation of Inferences from Persons' Responses and Performances as Scientific Inquiry into Score Meaning." _American Psychologist_, 50(9), 741-749.

    - **DOI**: 10.1037/0003-066X.50.9.741
    - **Summary**: Unified concept of validity emphasizing construct validity and consequences of assessment.
    - **Relevance**: Framework for validating EI assessment interpretations.

15. **Likert, R. (1932).** "A Technique for the Measurement of Attitudes." _Archives of Psychology_, 22(140), 5-55.
    - **Summary**: Introduced Likert scale methodology for attitude measurement.
    - **Relevance**: Theoretical basis for 1-5 rating scale used in Soul Sense.

## Applications in Mental Health

### EI, Depression, and Mental Health Outcomes

16. **Martins, A., Ramalho, N., & Morin, E. (2010).** "A Comprehensive Meta-Analysis of the Relationship Between Emotional Intelligence and Health." _Personality and Individual Differences_, 49(6), 554-564.

    - **DOI**: 10.1016/j.paid.2010.05.029
    - **Summary**: Meta-analysis showing moderate negative correlation between EI and mental health problems including depression.
    - **Relevance**: Empirical support for EI-mental health connection targeted by Soul Sense.

17. **Ciarrochi, J., Deane, F. P., & Anderson, S. (2002).** "Emotional Intelligence Moderates the Relationship Between Stress and Mental Health." _Personality and Individual Differences_, 32(2), 197-209.

    - **DOI**: 10.1016/S0191-8869(01)00012-5
    - **Summary**: Higher EI associated with better mental health outcomes and stress management.
    - **Relevance**: Theoretical justification for using EI as mental health indicator.

18. **Extremera, N., & Fernández-Berrocal, P. (2006).** "Emotional Intelligence as Predictor of Mental, Social, and Physical Health in University Students." _The Spanish Journal of Psychology_, 9(1), 45-51.

    - **DOI**: 10.1017/S1138741600005965
    - **Summary**: Longitudinal study showing EI predicts mental health outcomes over time.
    - **Relevance**: Supports predictive validity of EI assessments for mental health.

19. **Fernández-Berrocal, P., & Extremera, N. (2006).** "Emotional Intelligence: A Theoretical and Empirical Review of Its First 15 Years of History." _Psicothema_, 18(Suppl), 7-12.

    - **ISSN**: 0214-9915
    - **Summary**: Comprehensive review of EI research including mental health applications.
    - **Relevance**: Context for EI assessment in psychological well-being.

20. **Downey, L. A., Johnston, P. J., Hansen, K., Birney, J., & Stough, C. (2010).** "Investigating the Mediating Effects of Emotional Intelligence and Coping on Problem Behaviours in Adolescents." _Australian Journal of Psychology_, 62(1), 20-29.
    - **DOI**: 10.1080/00049530903312873
    - **Summary**: EI mediates relationship between stress and problematic behaviors in youth.
    - **Relevance**: Supports EI assessment for adolescent populations (Soul Sense targets ages 12+).
